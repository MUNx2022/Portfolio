Sofia Carolina Quintanilla Galindo

Engineering Physics · Modeling & Simulation · Research & Design

I am an Engineering Physics student at Tecnológico de Monterrey, focused on experimental design, computational modeling, and the development of real physical systems. I see physics as a way of understanding nature through its most fundamental principles, and engineering as the means to turn that understanding into solutions that can be built, measured, and used in the real world.

I work at the intersection of simulation and experimentation, using tools such as Python, MATLAB, and SolidWorks together with manufacturing, instrumentation, and laboratory testing. I am especially interested in biomimetics as a bridge between science and engineering, applying natural principles to the design of mechanical systems, materials, and energy technologies.

I am currently part of the Monterrey Rocket Lab as Propulsion Lead, where I design and test solid rocket motors, and a member of the Formula SAE team in the Powertrain area, working on structural analysis, composite materials, and mechanical integration. I am seeking research opportunities where I can combine applied physics, simulation, and hands-on experimentation to develop technologies with real-world impact.
